Product History

The domestic cat is believed to have evolved from the Near Eastern wildcat, whose range covers vast portions of the Middle East westward to the Atlantic coast of Africa. Between 70,000 and 100,000 years ago the animal gave rise to the genetic lineage that eventually produced all domesticated cats, having diverged from the Near Eastern wildcat around 8,000 BC in the Middle East.

Product Life Span

The average lifespan for a domestic cat is 15 years. Spayed and neutered cats live much longer than cats who are unaltered.

Home Installation

Preparing Your Home

It is very important to make preparations before your new cat enters your home. Some cats may be less demanding than others. However, most new cats will expect some basic requirements to be met upon entering your home for the first time.

Installation Requirements

  • The home must be a clean environment.
  • Do not try to tell your new cat which room belongs to them. Your new cat will claim their rooms without your assistance. WARNING: Be prepared to find out that your room now belongs to your new cat.
  • There must be a designated area for your new cat's food bowls. Most cats will allow you to choose this space.

Interior Maintenance

Your cat should have access to food and water at all times. It is best to keep a steady supply of fuel accessible.

Exterior Maintenance

Your cat has self-grooming protocols already installed. There is no reason to ever bath your cat. Doing so could result in an angry cat. You do not want to see your cat when they are angry.
